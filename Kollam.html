KOLLAM-TRAVEL GUIDE

Kollam is the centre of the country’s cashew trading and processing industry.This historic town is home to the celebrated Ashtamudi Lake, making it a gateway to the breath-taking backwaters of Kerala. The eight-hour boat trip between Kollam and Alappuzha is the longest and most enchanting experience on the backwaters of Kerala.

How to Reach Kollam:

By road:You can easily get regular buses to Kollam from other major cities of the country.

By rail:Kollam is well connected to other major cities of the country via regular trains.

By air: Nearest airport is Thiruvananthapuram International Airport.Kollam- 51 km away – Thiruvananthapuram International Airport (TRV).

Places to visit in Kollam:
  1. Jatayu Adventure Center

    2. The destination is a masterpiece combination of artistry, mythology, technology, culture, adventure, leisure and wellness put together to give every visitor a spellbinding experience. A complete feast to your eyes, the world’s largest bird sculpture is the recreation of Mr. Rajiv Anchal’s years of aspiration.

  2. Thenmala Ecotourism Centre

    3. Thenmala is India’s first planned eco-tourism destination.Thenmala is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site. A government-managed facility, this unique destination offers great opportunities for adventure and leisure, along with being one with nature.

  3. VPalaruvi Waterfalls

    4. 75 km from Kollam, Palaruvi, (which means stream of milk), makes its way down the rocks from a height of 300 ft. A sought-after picnic spot, the scenic beauty of this place is enhanced by hills, valleys and waterfalls.
