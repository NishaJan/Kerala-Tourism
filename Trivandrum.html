THIRUVANANTHAPURAM-TRAVEL GUIDE

Trivandrum or Thiruvananthapuram is the capital city of Kerala. Located on the western coastal regions of India, this metropolis is also the IT hub of God’s Own Country and one of the most sought after tourist destinations in Kerala.The city is built across seven of the most captivating hills and is dotted with several pious temples and religious sites. Though the city witness a number of rulers in its past, it has endured its beauty and liveliness in the most splendid ways, and has become of the highest visited tourist destinations in Kerala.

How to Reach Thiruvananthapuram:

By road:The capital city of Kerala enjoys a very decent connectivity with other major South Indian cities

By rail:Thiruvananthapuram has its own railway station which is well-connected to some of the major destinations across India. Trivandrum Central is also known to be the largest and the busiest railway station in the entire of Kerala.

By air: The nearest International airports is Trivandrum International Airport(7Km) and Vizhinjam International Seaport (22.9km).

Places to visit in Thiruvananthapuram:
  1. Sree Padmanabha Swami Temple

    2. The origin of the Temple of Sree Padmanabhaswamy is lost in antiquity. It is not possible to determine with any exactitude, from any reliable historical documents or other sources as to when and by whom the original idol of Sree Padmanabhaswamy was consecrated.The Temple has references in Epics and Puranas..The principal deity Vishnu is enshrined in the “Anantha Shayanam” posture, the eternal yogic sleep on the serpent Adisheshan.Sree Padmanabhaswamy is the tutelary deity of the royal family of Travancore.

  2. Veli Tourist Village

    3. Located at the confluence point of the Arabian Sea and the Veli Lake, this village is known for its bountiful beauty. While visiting the Veli hamlet, visitors can indulge in boating, village tour, and beach activities.

  3. Shanghumukham Beach

    4. This quaint and equally charming beach is located away from the humdrums of city-life. Cossetted by the azure water and playful sand, it is a perfect place to sit back and relax while enjoying the pristine beauty of Mother Nature.

  4. Kovalam beach

    5. (16 km from Thiruvananthapuram)This internationally renowned seaside destination has been a favourite haunt of tourists since the 1930s. Just a short drive away from the State capital, Kovalam is easily accessible. It encompasses three adjacent crescent-shaped beaches. Lighthouse Beach, Eve's or Howah Beach and the Ashok Beach .Golden sands, miles of shimmering sea, rocky promontories constantly caressed by the sensuous surf and brilliant sunshine, make this place truly sensational.

  5. Neyyer Dam

    6. Situated at the foothills of the mighty Western Ghats, this dam is among the highest visited tourist attractions in Trivandrum. Adventure junkies can also visit this dam and indulge in exciting activities like boating and mountaineering.
