KASARAGOD-TRAVEL GUIDE

The northernmost district of Kerala, Kasaragod is renowned for its hills, forts and water bodies. The coir and handloom industry here are extremely famous around the world. The place is also popular for its ancient temples and rich biodiversity.

How to Reach Kasaragod:

By road:An excellent network of roads connects Kasaragod to major towns in Kerala, Karnataka and Tamil Nadu.

By rail:The major Railway stations are Trikaripur, Cheruvathur, Nileshwar, Kanhangad, Kasargod, Kumbla, Uppla and Manjeshwar.Almost all Express trains will stop in these stations.

By air: No airport in Kasargod District.Nearby airports are Mangalore (69 km) & Kannur Airport 94km.

Places to visit in Kasaragod:
  1. Bekal Fort

    2. Bekal – Bekal Fort, is the largest fort in Kerala, situated at Bekal and it is 65 km from Mangalore city spreading over 40 acres.

  2. Ranipuram Hill Station

    3. (85 km from Kasaragod) Situated 750 m above sea level. Ranipuram is famous for its trekking trails and varied vegetation,evergreen shola Woods, monsoon forests and grasslands.

  3. Valiyaparamba Backwaters

    4. Separated from the mainland by backwaters, Valiyaparamba fishing village is an island with the Arabian Sea as one of the borders. Located about 30 km from Bekal, Valiyaparamba backwaters is one of the most picturesque backwater boroughs in Kerala. The island located in River Tejeswini, is known for its natural beauty.
